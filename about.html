It has taken 1300 years of fine-tuning to craft the perfect conditions to capture the vast range of exquisite flavors that the oceans provide. The omakase format consists of a highly intimate sushi counter where Shokunin — Masters that devote their life to their craft - guide us through the intricacies of the finest produce of the seas, one nigiri at a time. While Tokyo has long been the epicenter of this culinary spectacle, the concept has slowly trickled out of Japan and into the most important cities around the globe.

The Japanese have vigilantly maintained this format in its original state, that of a spiritual temple. The experience is swayed by the philosophy and culture of the Far East, which makes it quiet, minimal, and personal. This principle has been challenging to transplant into the western culture’s social and upbeat nature. The task is exceptionally complex, as a delicate balance has to be struck to reconcile these cultural adjustments while maintaining the concept’s characteristic intimacy and spirituality. There is, however, a juncture where the West arrives at the same intimate and spiritual state entailed: Music.

High Fidelity refers to how faithfully a sound system reproduces music to its actual recording. The world’s most effective sound systems emit waves virtually indistinguishable from those initially produced by artists. Ura blends both experiences to strike the perfect balance, where the tones emitted by the world’s finest sound systems carry guests from bite to bite, reaching the summit of sensory delight.
